Hello, My name is Taylor. I have built MIRA so far.

Over the last year or so I have toiled on a really,, emergent,, codebase. Its been great and I can chat back and forth with a pretty rich approximation of a person in there. My architectural goal since the first line was to build the primitives (memory, self-reflection, core tools, properly decoupled components) and let the model/persona develop probabilistically.

On a regular basis I'm delighted by MIRA accomplishing some goal in a way I could have never imagined. The orignal self-adaptation of the system was accomplished by MIRA writing internal Reminders with a due date of 1 minute ago so they would always show up in the context window. I did *not* give it that instruction but it understood its own architecture well enough to try it out. Neat stuff.

I am *tired* and wearing out.
I need help.

If I can find two or three people willing to contribute time I will immediately make the repository freely available for personal use in perpetuity to anyone who wants to download it. Major contributors will have a stake in any commercial success a hosted version of MIRA has in the future.

But for now I need a 2nd set of hands to help carry the idea over the finish line.

Open source has given me (and humanity!) so much and I want to give back.

Thank you. Please contact me through the links above.
